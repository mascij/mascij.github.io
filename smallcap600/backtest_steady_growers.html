What is the Steady Score?

The Steady Score is a composite metric that identifies stocks with reliable, consistent growth over time. It combines two key factors:

Steady Score = (Consistency √ó 60%) + (Stability √ó 40%)

Components Explained:

1. Consistency Score (60% weight):

  • Measures what percentage of months had positive returns
  • Calculated over the lookback period (6, 12, or 18 months)
  • Formula: (Positive Months √∑ Total Months) √ó 100
  • Example: 9 out of 12 positive months = 75% consistency
  • Higher is better - shows reliable upward trajectory

2. Stability Score (40% weight):

  • Measures how predictable the returns are (inverse of volatility)
  • Based on standard deviation of monthly returns
  • Formula: 100 - min(Volatility, 100)
  • Example: 8% volatility = 92 stability score
  • Higher is better - shows smooth, predictable gains

Why This Matters:

Stocks with high Steady Scores demonstrate repeated monthly gains with low volatility.
